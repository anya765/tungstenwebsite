Remote Areas in Developed Countries

Homes in developed countries that are not currently serviced by the grid can take advantage of wireless electricity technology. Especially in mountaneous landscapes where wired infrastrucutre becomes exponentially more expensive to build, electricity can be transferred through pylons over long distances for even a small number of people.
Maximizing the impact of small scale projects

Homes in Developing Countries

With Tungsten, more people have the ability to invest in renewable energy projects and sell electricity into the network. Small scale projects, which often have high installation and maintainance costs, can be put together to produce more energy and Tungsten Pylons and Antennas can serve as the infrastrucutre needed to get electricity to virtually anywhere.
Enabling new energy investments

Creating incentives for remote energy
production

Today, one of the biggest challenges with renewable energy investments is getting electricity to consumers who need it. With Tungsten, producers can find the most optimal places for renewable energy projects and leave energy transmission to the network. Incentives built into the network encourage people to buy antennas to facilitate energy transmission in exchange for crypto rewards.
